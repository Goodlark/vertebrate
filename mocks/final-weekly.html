Physical AI & Humanoids
Launch

Figure Puts a Humanoid on BMW’s Line, and a Question on the Table

// The Verge · Spartanburg, S.C.

Why it matters. This is the first time a humanoid robot has taken real, repeated factory work at scale rather than posing for a launch video — the metric has shifted from “can it walk” to “can it pay for itself.” For anyone who shapes narratives around automation, it also detonates the question the industry keeps deferring: the plant employs three thousand people, and no one in Spartanburg has said aloud what the new hire does to that number.

Funding

Physical Intelligence Raises $400 Million to Teach Robots the Obvious

// Bloomberg

Why it matters. The half-billion-dollar bet rests on a single idea: that one “foundation model” can drive many different robot bodies, the way one language model can write in many voices. If it holds, the expensive part of building any robot — the brain — becomes shared infrastructure, and the cost of the whole field drops. If it doesn’t, it will be the most costly reminder yet that atoms are harder than tokens.

Driverless
Launch

Waymo Merges Onto the Freeway, at Last

// TechCrunch · Phoenix

Why it matters. Freeway driving has been the industry’s stubborn last mile — high speed, no margin, exactly where a hesitant robot becomes a dangerous one. Doing it in live Phoenix traffic nudges the driverless car from “novelty in a fenced-off grid” toward something that could actually replace a commute. Every rival now owes an answer to a single question: why does your car still take the surface streets?
